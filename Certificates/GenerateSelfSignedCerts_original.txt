pem(Optional) Self-Generate and Self-Sign X.509 Certificates with OpenSSL
When you're done, copy the CRT and PFX files to the same folder as your SimpleAzureIoTCerts executable, or specify the full path to them when prompted.

### This command generates a "primary" cert with output of primary.crt and primary.pfx ###
openssl req -newkey rsa:2048 -nodes -keyout primary.key -x509 -days 365 -out primary.crt
openssl pkcs12 -export -out primary.pfx -inkey primary.key -in primary.crt


### This command generates a "secondary" cert with output of secondary.crt and secondary.pfx ###
openssl req -newkey rsa:2048 -nodes -keyout secondary.key -x509 -days 365 -out secondary.crt
openssl pkcs12 -export -out secondary.pfx -inkey secondary.key -in secondary.crt

### To get thumbprint of those certificates ###
openssl x509 -noout -fingerprint -sha1 -inform pem -in [certificate-file.crt]

### To print out the thumbprints ###
openssl x509 -out thumbprint1.txt -fingerprint -sha1 -inform pem -in primary.crt
openssl x509 -out thumbprint2.txt -fingerprint -sha1 -inform pem -in secondary.crt

### To get the RSA Key File from the private key ###
openssl rsa -in private_key.pem -out primary_rsa.key
